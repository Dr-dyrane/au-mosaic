# The house's camera: prompt kit

Every frame the site wears was generated for the maison, eye-gated,
and served from public/media. This kit keeps new frames in the same
family. Copy a prompt, generate at the largest size offered, then the
eye gate decides: if it would not hang in the showroom, it does not
enter the book.

One law before the prompts. Generated frames live in the dream lane:
environments, moods, concept studies, always labelled as such where a
customer could mistake them for delivered work. The proof lane
(finished projects, the trust stones) waits for real photographs.
A generated image wearing the clothes of a completed job is the one
frame that could cost the house its name.

## The house DNA

Append this to every prompt below.

> Photorealistic architectural photography, shot on medium format,
> 35mm lens, natural light only, no people, no text, no watermarks.
> Lagos latitude light: warm, heavy, golden at dusk, white and soft
> by morning. Materials read true: glass mosaic shows its glass,
> grout lines are honest, water has weight. Composition calm and
> frontal or gently elevated, generous negative space, nothing busy.
> Palette anchored in deep teals, aquamarine, warm sand, brass
> accents. Luxury-hotel stillness, editorial restraint, no HDR glow,
> no lens flare, no oversaturation.

Two suns per scene. Generate each frame twice:

- Night: "blue hour, lamps just lit, water holding the last light,
  long soft shadows."
- Day: "mid-morning, soft white sun, gentle shadows, the honest
  daylight version of the same scene."

Portrait frames (piece pages, environments) at 4:5 or 3:4. Wide
frames (heroes, journal headers) at 21:9 or 16:9. Nothing square.

## Environments (the dream lane)

The four that exist: the Dusk Villa, the Infinity Terrace, the
Private Hammam, the Dark Bath. Candidates to grow the set, one
prompt each plus the DNA block:

1. The Courtyard Fountain. "A private Lagos courtyard at blue hour,
   a circular fountain lined in iridescent glass mosaic, water
   overflowing a brass lip, palm shadows on warm sand walls."
2. The Roof Pool. "A rooftop lap pool above a city at dusk, deep
   midnight-blend mosaic, the skyline out of focus beyond the far
   edge, one line of underwater light."
3. The Garden Wall. "A garden wall carrying a large abstract mosaic
   mural in teal and gold, dusk light raking across the tesserae,
   clipped hedges below, nothing else."
4. The Spa Corridor. "A narrow spa corridor, floor-to-ceiling stone
   mosaic in warm sand tones, a strip of daylight at the far end,
   steam softening the air."

## Piece photography (the stockroom's window)

For a piece record's night and day slots, the subject is the material
itself, close enough to touch:

> "A swimming pool wall and waterline seen from just above the
> surface, tiled in [COLOUR/BLEND NAME] glass mosaic, ripples
> bending the grout lines, [night/day sun line], nothing in frame
> but water, tile, and light."

Replace [COLOUR/BLEND NAME] with the piece: classic pool blues, aqua
and turquoise, deep midnight, gold and metallic, and so on. Upload
through the piece record; the window refines and previews it in both
suns before a customer ever sees it.

## Journal headers (wide, quiet)

> "A wide, almost abstract frame of [water over mosaic / a hand
> holding one glass sheet / stacked mosaic sheets in a dim
> stockroom], 21:9, heavy negative space on the [left/right] for
> the headline to breathe, [night/day sun line]."

## The gate, unchanged

Generate large, view at full size, and ask the three questions: does
the light fall one way; does the material read true at arm's length;
would a customer feel lied to standing in the showroom? Two of three
is a no. Then compress to a few hundred kilobytes before it enters
public/media, and give every frame its day pair when it earns one.
